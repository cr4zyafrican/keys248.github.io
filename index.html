About

I love both carbon based life and silicon based life to equal extents. My mind is constantly blown by biology facts. Isn't it crazy how for every human cell in our body we have the same amount of non-human things living on or in us?1 Gross but super cool. Sometimes I'll code for 6 hours straight and feel like only an hour passed. Also the Bash command line interface makes me feel safe. That's why I'm a Biologist and a Computer Scientist. I'm combining both passions through an open source, computational biology research project as an undergrad at NYU. Yes, I love how long that description is. Getting three minors inspired my hobby of collecting degrees, so I'm planning on a masters before applying to medical school. Catch me in the library.

1. Sender, R., Fuchs, S., & Milo, R. (2016). Revised Estimates for the Number of Human and Bacteria Cells in the Body. PLoS Biology, 14(8), e1002533. http://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pbio.1002533
