About

I consider myself a Biologist and a Computer Scientist. I am completing my Bachelor degree in Biology with a minors in Computer Science, Chemistry, and Hebrew & Judaic Studies. My current research combines biological theory with computer science tools as it contributes to an open source project by adding the ability to measure the growth rate of cells. I am a pre-medical student with ambitions of becoming a physician with a technology focus. Home is in California but I live in New York while I finish up my senior year of undergraduate work at New York University.

Involvement

Most of my extracurricular time is spent with the BUGS Open Source club at NYU. I was one of the founding members and currently hold the position of Treasurer and Communications. We promote the philosophies of open source and get students involved with practicing open source through facilitated, small scale projects run by passionate students. Our website can be found at bugs-nyu.github.io.

My other club commitment on NYU campus is as Treasurer for the Red Cross club. I am also an ongoing volunteer in the Neurology unit of the New York Presbyterian, Weill Cornell Hospital.
